Toscana Soup

    Ingredients

  • 1 lb spicy Italian ground sausage

  • 4 tbsp butter
  • 1/2 white onion

  • 1 tbsp minced garlic
  • 6 cups chicken broth

  • 2 cups water
  • 5 yellow potatoes

  • 3 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 4 cups chopped kale

  • 1/2 cup chopped bacon

    • Instructions


    In a large pot saute sausage for five minutes until browned. Use a slotted spoon to transfer sausage to a plate and set aside. In the same pot, add butter and saute onions over medium heat until translucent. Add garlic and saute for another minute until fragrant. Add chicken broth, water, potatoes, salt and pepper and bring it to a boil. Boil until potatoes are tender. Stir in kale, and heavy cream. Add sausage. Taste and add salt and pepper if needed. Serve garnished with grated cheese.

Aperol Spritz

    Ingredients

  • 1 wine glass

  • 3 ounces prosecco

  • 2 ounces Aperol

  • 1 ounce club soda

  • 1 orange slice

    • Instructions


    Add the prosecco, Aperol, and club soda to a wine glass filled with ice. Give the cocktail a quick stir and garnish with an orange slice. Enjoy!

Cacio Pepe

    Ingredients

  • 8 ounces dry spaghettie pasta

  • 3 strips smoked bacon - cut into small peices

  • 4 tbsp unsaled butter

  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms

  • 3 /4 cup grated parmesan cheese

  • 1 tsp cracked black pepper

    • Instructions


    Cook spaghetti according to instructions on packaging. Reserve 2/3 cup of pasta water for sauce. Place bacon in a large nonstick skillet and cook five minutes over medium heat until tnder. Add mushrooms and butter to pan, cook for four minutes, occassionally stirring.
    Add spaghetti to pan and stir in reserved pasta water and shaved parmesan cheese. Continue to stir over medium-low heat until spaghetti is warmed throughout and cheese is melted. Top with freshly cracked black pepper and serve.

Cannoli Bites

    Ingredients

  • 2 refrigerated pie crust

  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1/4 tsp cinnamon

  • chopped pistachios
  • chocolate chips

  • 15 ounces ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup powdered sugar

  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 2 tbsp sugar

  • 1 tsp orange zest

    • Instructions


    Roll out the pie crusts on a flat surface. Whisk sugar and cinnamon together, sprinkle over pie crusts. Use 2-inch cookie cutter to cut circles. Gently press each side into muffin tin to form "cups". Bake at 400 for 10 minutes. Allow to cool. Prepare filling by mixing ricotta cheese and powdered sugar on medium speed for a minute, then high speed until smooth. Add remaining ingredients and mix well. Fill pie crust "cups" and top with chocolate chips or crushed pistachios.
